Week 7 Final — May 11–17, 2026
CASCADES MAKE IT FIVE STRAIGHT AND ACQUIRE A CLOSER; THE BANK WINS THREE RUNNING; NEWBERRY'S NUMBERS DEMAND AN EXPLANATION
The Troutdale Cascades are 6-1 with the league's best record, most points, and now a legitimate closer in Tanner Scott. The BANK of Texas has won three straight on the strength of the league's best rotation duo. And the Newberry Indians have 3,005 fantasy points, a 3-4 record, and a statistical case that the standings are simply lying about how good they are.
By the Longball Gazette Wire Staff
League Headquarters — Sunday, May 17, 2026

The Troutdale Cascades are the best team in MLBLONGBALL and the numbers have stopped being subtle about it. Six wins. One loss. Three thousand, two hundred and fifty-eight cumulative fantasy points — the highest total in the league. A five-game winning streak. And now Tanner Scott, a legitimate ninth-inning closer, arrived Saturday night via a $205 Vickrey bid for one real dollar, solving the one structural vacancy that has defined this franchise since Opening Day. The Cascades are not quietly good. They are loudly, systematically, relentlessly good. The rest of the field is measuring themselves against a standard that keeps moving upward.

The BANK of Texas has won three consecutive games. That sentence deserves to sit on its own for a moment, because three weeks ago the BANK was 1-4 and the conversation about their season was already turning toward what went wrong. Now they are 4-3, tied with Sidd Finch for the RED Division lead, and Cristopher Sanchez (48.5 pts) and Bailey Ober (44 pts) have both finished in the league's top four starters in back-to-back weeks. JJ Bleday at 7.83 FP/G is giving the lineup the third offensive force that Soto and Acuña have been waiting for since February. The BANK of Texas, written off in April, is a legitimate second-half story.

And then there are the Newberry Indians — the league's most productive team that nobody is talking about because the wins column doesn't reflect it. Three thousand and five cumulative fantasy points. A 3-4 record. More FPtsF than Goldy Goldy Gone. More than the Desert Rats. More than nine of twelve franchises in this league. The Indians led the entire league in innings pitched, strikeouts, and saves-holds in Week 7, and lost to Sidd Finch by five points. The gap between what the Newberry Indians produce and what the scoreboard credits them for is the central analytical story of the 2026 MLBLONGBALL season.

The Free Agent Emergency That Nobody Addressed

Gavin Sheets posted 56.5 fantasy points at 9.42 FP/G this week. He is a free agent. He was unclaimed from Monday through Sunday while teams in this league executed contested Vickrey auctions for $101 and cycled through hundreds of combined roster transactions. Keibert Ruiz — 40.5 points, 10.12 FP/G, catcher eligibility — is also unclaimed. The two players combine for 97 points this week on the wire. The first team to claim both before Week 8 opens wins the waiver wire by forfeit.

The Desert Rats at 5-2: Still Dangerous, Still Churning

The Desert Rats dropped a 21.83-point decision to Goldy Goldy Gone and slide to 5-2 in the BLUE Division — still tied with Goldy for second behind the Cascades, still fully in the playoff race. Sasaki (37 pts in his debut) and Burger (43.5 pts) performed. Carroll was simply better on the day. The Rats remain a genuine contender with Ohtani, Tucker, and a transaction wire that never sleeps. Week 8 at home vs. the BANK of Texas — hot, confident, armed with Sanchez and Ober — is the most important game the commissioner has played in a month. How they respond after a setback will tell us more about this team than any win did.
