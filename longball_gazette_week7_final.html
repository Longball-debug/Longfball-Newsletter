Week 7 Final — May 11–17, 2026
GOLDY ENDS THE RATS' PERFECTION; CASCADES MAKE IT FIVE STRAIGHT; THE WIRE LEAVES A FORTUNE UNCLAIMED
The Desert Rats' perfect record lasted seven weeks before Goldy Goldy Gone took it apart by 21.83 points. The Cascades won their fifth straight and acquired Tanner Scott. The BANK of Texas is on a three-game heater. And Gavin Sheets — 56.5 points, 9.42 FP/G — spent the week as a free agent while twelve teams cycled through combined hundreds of transactions without noticing him.
By the Longball Gazette Wire Staff
League Headquarters — Sunday, May 17, 2026

It was a 21.83-point loss. In the context of a full season, that margin is survivable. But the Desert Rats' 5-2 record arriving at Week 8 carries a different weight than 6-1 would have, and the team that inflicted it — Goldy Goldy Gone, now 5-2 themselves and very much alive in the BLUE Division race — did it with Corbin Carroll (49.5 pts, 8.25 FP/G) leading an offense that simply produced more points when they mattered. Roki Sasaki delivered 37 points in his Rats debut. Jake Burger contributed 43.5. It wasn't enough. Goldy's floor proved higher than the Rats' ceiling on this particular week, and the commissioner's squad has its first genuine wound heading into a matchup with the BANK of Texas that may define the second half.

The Troutdale Cascades have now won five consecutive games and sit alone atop the entire league with 3,258.833 cumulative fantasy points. Tanner Scott arrived Saturday night via a $205 bid for one real dollar — a legitimate closing arm, the one structural gap that has defined the Cascades since Opening Day — and Zack Wheeler (58.17 pts, 29.08 FP/G) delivered the week's best pitching performance. This roster now has an answer at every position. The question is whether anyone in the field can find a question the Cascades can't answer.

The statistical story of the week — and perhaps of the season — belongs to the Newberry Indians, who have scored 3,005.501 cumulative fantasy points while sitting at 3-4. That is more FPtsF than Goldy Goldy Gone. More than the Desert Rats. More than nine of twelve teams in this league. The Indians led the entire league in innings pitched, strikeouts, and saves-holds this week and lost to Sidd Finch by five points. The gap between the Newberry Indians' production and their record is the most jarring disconnect in MLBLONGBALL and it has been building for seven weeks.

The Free Agent Emergency That Nobody Addressed

Gavin Sheets posted 56.5 fantasy points at 9.42 FP/G this week. He is a free agent. He was unclaimed from Monday through Sunday while teams in this league executed contested Vickrey auctions for $101 and cycled through hundreds of combined roster transactions. Keibert Ruiz — 40.5 points, 10.12 FP/G, catcher eligibility — is also unclaimed. The two players combine for 97 points this week on the wire. The first team to claim both before Week 8 opens wins the waiver wire by forfeit.

The BANK of Texas Has a Three-Game Win Streak and Some Explaining to Do

The BANK of Texas is 4-3, tied for first in the RED Division, and coming off their best back-to-back pitching weeks of the season. Cristopher Sanchez (48.5 pts) and Bailey Ober (44 pts) are two of the four best starters in the league right now when both are performing. JJ Bleday (47 pts, 7.83 FP/G) is giving the lineup the third offensive force it has needed since February. Week 8 at the Desert Rats is the defining game of the BANK's resurgence. Win there, and the "BANK is back" narrative becomes a genuine second-half story.

The First Loss and What It Means
By Joe Falls  ·  J.G. Taylor Spink Award Winner, 2001  ·  The Longball Gazette

I've been waiting seven weeks for the Desert Rats to lose a game. Not because I wanted it. Because the story of an undefeated team is always incomplete until the first loss arrives and you find out what the franchise is made of. Now we know. Week 8 at home against the BANK of Texas — three-game win streak, Sanchez and Ober pitching like aces, a fully activated lineup — is the character test. The first loss is never the story. The response to the first loss is.

The Rats lost by 21 points to Goldy Goldy Gone and it was a closer game than that margin suggests. Sasaki delivered 37 points in his first start after Saturday's claim. Burger posted 43.5. Carroll's 49.5 was the difference — a performance that required the rest of Goldy's roster to simply not blow the lead, which they didn't. The Rats are 5-2. They have the tools. Ohtani at 5.5 FP/G as a starter is a number that needs to change before the BANK game, or the commissioner's team is going to be in a real fight at home.

Now: the Newberry Indians. I want to be direct about this. Three thousand and five fantasy points. Three wins and four losses. In my forty years covering sports I have seen teams lose playoff games they deserved to win, seen franchises build correctly and watch a single bad bounce eliminate everything. The Newberry Indians' 2026 season is the fantasy baseball version of all of it simultaneously. They led the league in innings pitched, strikeouts, and saves-holds this week. They lost by five points. That is not a talent problem. That is not a process problem. That is the universe being specifically unkind to a team that is doing everything right, and I have run out of patient ways to describe it.

The Troutdale Cascades are 6-1. Their FPtsA is 2,384 — the lowest in the league by a margin that should embarrass every other pitching staff in MLBLONGBALL. Tanner Scott arrived on Saturday for one dollar. Wheeler delivered 58 points. Five-game win streak. This is the team everyone said would fade. I was wrong to pick Goldy over them in our preseason panel. I remain comfortable with Goldy's long-term prospects. But the Cascades are the standard right now and the standard has been consistently excellent for seven weeks.

I want to say one more thing about Kyle Bradish. Goldy paid one hundred and one real dollars for a starting pitcher through the Vickrey system. One hundred and one. Someone else bid one hundred and lost by one dollar. That is not a transaction. That is a statement of intent from a franchise that has decided this is the season and is willing to pay to prove it. The BANK spent two dollars in February on two future Hall of Famers. Goldy spent one hundred and one dollars in May for a starting pitcher. Both are legitimate strategies. Only one of them involves triple digits and a contested auction. The arms race is real and Goldy just confirmed they're in it to win it.

Gavin Sheets: 56.5 points as a free agent. I don't need to say more than that. The first team to claim him wins this week's wire by default and anyone who didn't is going to spend Monday morning reading this column and understanding exactly what opportunity they missed.

Three Thousand Points, Four Wins, and the Nature of Justice
By Mitch Albom  ·  Detroit Free Press  ·  The Longball Gazette

There is a number in this week's standings that keeps returning to me. Three thousand and five. That is how many fantasy points the Newberry Indians have accumulated in seven weeks. They have more cumulative production than Goldy Goldy Gone. More than the Desert Rats. More than nine of twelve franchises in this league. And they sit at 3-4, coming off a five-point loss to Sidd Finch in a week where they led every major pitching category in the league.

I have spent a career writing about the relationship between effort and outcome — in sports, in life, in the quiet spaces between the two. The Newberry Indians' 2026 season is a lesson in how that relationship can break down without warning and without explanation. They are not underperforming. They are not mismanaged. They are doing what the system rewards and watching the scoreboard fail to agree. That gap — between process and result — is one of the quieter injustices in a sport that is usually too loud to make room for them.

The Troutdale Cascades tell a different story. Six wins. The league's highest cumulative points. The lowest points allowed differential. And now Tanner Scott, arrived Saturday for one dollar on a $205 bid, potentially closing the one structural gap that has defined this franchise since Opening Day. I picked the Cascades in our championship panel. The roster I described in that panel is now more complete than it was when I picked them. I remain comfortable with the selection.

What happened with Roki Sasaki this week is worth examining carefully. Sidd Finch dropped Sasaki on Tuesday. The Desert Rats claimed him on Saturday. Sasaki posted 37 points in his debut. Sidd Finch won their matchup by five points. Had Sasaki remained in Finch's rotation for one more week, the margin becomes 40+ in Finch's favor. The wire is not merely a source of players. It is a source of narrative. Every drop is a door. Every claim is a choice about which story gets told.

I want to note what Gavin Sheets represents beyond the 56.5 points. He represents the gap between information and action that exists in every competitive system. The information was available. The data was clear. The action — a simple claim — was not taken. In fantasy baseball, as in all competitive endeavors, the difference between winning and losing is often not talent but attention. The managers who claim Sheets before Week 8 opens have made that attention real. The managers who did not will look at this number all week and know what it cost them.

The Desert Rats lost their first game. The response will define whether this is a team that learns from difficulty or a team that responds to it by making forty more transactions and hoping one of them is the answer. The tools are real. Ohtani, Tucker, Burger, Sasaki — these are legitimate assets. The question is whether the commissioner's management of those assets in Week 8 matches the quality of the players themselves. The first loss is a teacher. The question is whether the student is listening.
