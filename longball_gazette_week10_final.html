Week 10 Final — June 1–7, 2026
RATS SLAY THE STREAK; SIDD ERUPTS; NEWBERRY KEEPS ON GRINDING
Seven wins in a row for the Cascades. Gone. The Desert Rats walked into Troutdale, posted 388 points, and walked out with the biggest scalp of Scoring Period 10. Meanwhile Sidd Finch reminded everyone they can still bring the thunder, the Indians won their third straight, and the BANK and Gales staged what may have been the least exciting game in league history.
By the Longball Gazette Wire Staff
League Headquarters — Sunday, June 7, 2026

The Troutdale Cascades arrived at Week 10 riding the longest winning streak in MLBLONGBALL 2026 — seven consecutive victories, a 7-1 divisional record, and a cumulative fantasy points total (4,645.166) that made them look like a team that had simply decided to stop losing. The Desert Rats arrived carrying three losses and a bone to pick. When the dust settled Sunday evening, the Rats had posted 388 points to the Cascades’ 324.833 and sent the league’s hottest team home with their first L since what feels like a previous geological era. The Cascades fall to 8-2. The Rats climb to 7-3. The gap at the top of the standings is now one game.

If that was the headline, Sidd Finch supplied the exclamation point. The perpetually surprising, perpetually confounding Sidd Finch squad posted 468.667 points — the week’s highest total by a country mile — and dismantled the Midland Red Weasel Dogs 468.667 to 368.667. Jacob deGrom delivered 53 points. Sandy Alcantara added 48.5. The Finch pitching machine, which has been the most entertaining boom-or-bust story in this league all season, chose Week 10 to go full boom. The Dogs, for their part, got a 57.5-point masterpiece from Noah Cameron that deserved to be on a winning roster but wasn’t.

The Newberry Indians are on a three-game winning streak and nobody seems to want to talk about it. They beat Goldy Goldy Gone 396.667 to 365.333, methodically, professionally, and without drama — which is entirely on brand. Bryce Elder (35.83 pts) and Jackson Chourio (48.5 pts for the Indians) anchored a disciplined week that keeps Newberry at 6-4 and firmly in the playoff picture. Goldy drops to 6-4 on a two-game skid and is beginning to look like a team that needs to rediscover what made it dominant in the first season.

The BANK–Gales Game: A Special Kind of Bad

The BANK of Texas defeated the Troutdale Gales 287.833 to 252, which means that between them these two teams — both of whom have legitimate roster talent — combined for 539.833 total fantasy points. In a seven-day scoring period. For context, Sidd Finch alone scored 468 points. The BANK and Gales together scored 71 more points than one team. Gage Jump (50.67 pts) and Cristopher Sanchez (31.5 pts) were the BANK’s only significant contributors. For the Gales, Roki Sasaki (32 pts) was their lone top-10 performer — which tells you everything you need to know about where the 252 points came from. The BANK wins, extends their streak to two, and moves to 5-5. The Gales fall to 4-6 and desperately need to figure out why a roster with Mike Trout on it keeps posting 250-point weeks.

Sons Keep Rising; Scorpions Keep Struggling

The Sons of Pitches are 3-7. They have won three of their last four. Matt Chapman (56 pts, top hitter of the week) and Yordan Alvarez (49.5 pts) gave them the offensive firepower to beat the Tempe Scorpions 386.333 to 282.167. The Scorpions fall to 4-6 and have now been on the wrong side of enough close games to make their record look worse than their talent deserves. The Sons’ winning streak is real. Whether it’s sustainable is another question for another week.

The Troutdale Cascades had won seven straight. They had the best record in MLBLONGBALL. They had the most cumulative fantasy points in the league. They had Jacob Misiorowski on the mound posting 31.5 points at a 0.00 ERA. And they lost anyway, because the Desert Rats brought 388 points to a house that had been locked for seven weeks and broke the door down.

The commissioner’s squad was not dominant. There was no 57-point ace, no hitter going 10-for-25. What the Rats brought was volume and balance — consistent contributions across the lineup that added up to a number the Cascades simply couldn’t answer. Roki Sasaki (32 pts) was solid for Troutdale but the offense didn’t provide the kind of firepower that a 388-point week demands. The streak is over. The Cascades are still 8-2 and still the league leader. But their aura of invincibility has a crack in it, and the Rats are the team that put it there.

This is the matchup that changes the narrative heading into the second half. The Rats trail by one game in the standings. They just proved they can beat the best team in the league on the road. That is not nothing.

Sidd Finch scored 468.667 points. That is the highest weekly total in Scoring Period 10 by more than 75 points. Jacob deGrom went out and posted 53 points — 11 innings pitched, 14 strikeouts, 0.00 ERA, two quality starts. Sandy Alcantara delivered 48.5 on the back of 14 innings and a 2.57 ERA. Pete Crow-Armstrong (43.5 pts) anchored an offense that provided genuine support for once. This was Sidd Finch operating at something close to their theoretical ceiling, and the Midland Red Weasel Dogs happened to be standing in the way.

The cruel injustice here is that the Dogs did not have a bad week. Noah Cameron pitched 13 innings, struck out 15, posted a 0.69 ERA, and scored 57.5 fantasy points — the best individual pitching performance in the entire league this week. Hunter Goodman hit 48 points at catcher. The Dogs scored 368 points, which on most weeks wins a matchup. It didn’t win this one because Sidd Finch chose Week 10 to remember who they were in Week 1.

At 4-6, the Dogs’ season continues to be defined by having excellent components that don’t always add up to wins at the right time.

The Newberry Indians win. Again. Third consecutive victory, same formula that has been working all season: disciplined roster deployment, consistent pitching production, and an offense that doesn’t beat itself. Jackson Chourio (48.5 pts, 11-for-29, 2 HR, 8 RBI) was their offensive standout. Bryce Elder added 35.83 points on the mound. The Indians do not need to post 468-point weeks. They need to post 396-point weeks with the consistency of a Swiss watch, and that is exactly what they do.

Goldy Goldy Gone is skidding. Two losses in a row, 6-4 record, and an offense that is leaving points on the field. The cumulative FPts number (4,381.833) is actually higher than Newberry’s (4,202.834) — which tells you the record understates Goldy’s talent but the scheduling has been rough. Goldy needs to rediscover their Week 1 dominance before the playoff push tightens up.

The Streak Is Dead. Now What?
By Joe Falls  ·  J.G. Taylor Spink Award Winner, 2001  ·  The Longball Gazette

Seven wins in a row. That is what the Troutdale Cascades had walking into Week 10. Seven wins that had transformed them from a very good team into something the league had started treating as inevitable. And then the Desert Rats went to Troutdale and played 388 points of baseball and put the streak in the ground.

I want to be honest about what this means and what it doesn’t mean. The Cascades are still 8-2. They still have the best record in MLBLONGBALL. Their cumulative fantasy points total — 4,645 — is the highest in the league by 263 points. Misiorowski went out and posted 31.5 points at a 0.00 ERA. Roki Sasaki delivered 32 solid points. The Cascades didn’t fall apart. They had a week where the Rats were simply better. That happens to good teams. It happens to every good team eventually.

What it does mean is this: the Desert Rats are one game back, they are coming off the season’s most significant win, and they just added Corey Seager to a roster that already had Shohei Ohtani and Kyle Tucker. Adding an All-Star-caliber shortstop in June while sitting at 7-3 is the move of a team that has decided it is going to win this thing. The methodology — still too many transactions, still a roster identity that changes daily — is secondary to the result. Seven and three with momentum and a new shortstop. You cannot dismiss that.

Now let me say something about Sidd Finch, because 468 points demands acknowledgment. Jacob deGrom at 0.00 ERA. Sandy Alcantara at 2.57. Pete Crow-Armstrong at 43.5 points. That is a peak-performance week from a team whose season has been defined by alternating between excellent and mysterious. The five-five record doesn’t capture who Sidd Finch is on their best weeks. The question is whether their best weeks can be sequenced to align with the right opponents at the right time. In this format, timing is everything.

The thing that sticks with me from Week 10 is Noah Cameron of the Midland Red Weasel Dogs. Fifty-seven-and-a-half points. The week’s best individual pitching performance by any single arm in MLBLONGBALL. Thirteen innings, fifteen strikeouts, 0.69 ERA. And his team lost by 100 points because they happened to be playing Sidd Finch’s best week of the season. Cameron deserves better. The Dogs’ offense needs to look at that line and ask itself some hard questions about what it is doing every week while he’s out there pitching like that.

Troutdale Gales: 252 points. Mike Trout is on that roster. I have nothing more to add.

Seven Wins, One Loss, and What Endures
By Mitch Albom  ·  Detroit Free Press  ·  The Longball Gazette

A winning streak is a story we tell about a team when they are winning. It makes them feel permanent, like something that cannot be stopped except by time or injury or some greater force. Seven games in a row for the Troutdale Cascades had begun to feel that way — not just a streak but a statement about who this team is and what this season was going to be. And then the Desert Rats played 388 points of baseball and reminded us that in this league, every week is a new negotiation with the sport itself, and no team is exempt from that conversation.

The Cascades are still the best team in MLBLONGBALL by most objective measures. But what this week revealed is something worth examining: the gap between first and second place is one game. It was not one game in April. It was not one game in May. It is one game now because the Desert Rats have refused to accept the distance between themselves and the Cascades, and Week 10 is the proof.

I want to think about the Sons of Pitches for a moment, because their season is one of the more human stories in this league. Three wins in their last four games. Matt Chapman at 56 points, 8-for-21, three home runs, fourteen RBI — those are numbers that belong in a season highlight. Yordan Alvarez adding 49.5. Jake Bauers at 44.5. Three hitters in the top seven league-wide. A team at 3-7 doing this. There is something in that about perseverance that does not require a record to validate it.

The Newberry Indians keep winning and keep getting less credit than they deserve. Three consecutive victories. Jackson Chourio delivering 48.5 points against a Goldy squad that has been the preseason darling since February. Bryce Elder being Bryce Elder — quiet, reliable, exactly what he is supposed to be. The Indians do not need validation from the national conversation because the standings provide it. Six and four, three-game win streak, Red Division leaders. That is who they are.

And then there is Sidd Finch, at 468 points, operating at the precise intersection of everything this team is capable of when it works. deGrom at 0.00 ERA. Alcantara at 2.57. Crow-Armstrong turning in his best offensive week of the season. If Sidd Finch could be this team every week, the conversation about who wins MLBLONGBALL would be different. They cannot be this team every week. That has been the story of their season from the beginning — extraordinary ceilings, irregular floors, and a record that splits the difference between them.

The Troutdale Gales scored 252 points. Elly De La Cruz, who was supposed to be the centerpiece of their offensive identity, is not appearing on weekly leaderboards. The Gales have the talent to be a different team than their record suggests. They are running out of weeks to prove it.

Big Lou’s Corner: The Streak Is Dead and I Am Not Sorry
By Lou Palmieri, Baseball Lifer  ·  The Longball Gazette

SEVEN AND ONE. That’s what the Cascades are in their last eight games. I said the Desert Rats were champions in February. I said it in March. I said it in May. And here we are in June and the Rats just went into Troutdale — the house of seven consecutive wins — and posted 388 points and walked out with a victory that nobody gave them. NOBODY. The Cascades were supposed to cruise. The Cascades were supposed to extend the streak to eight. The Rats were supposed to lose politely and accept their position in second place. They did not do any of those things. They added Corey Seager and Brendan Donovan and they went to Troutdale and they WON.

Now I am not here to eulogize the Cascades. Eight and two is still an excellent record. Four thousand six hundred and forty-five cumulative points is still the best in the league. Misiorowski went out and pitched beautifully for them. They are fine. But they are now one game ahead of a Desert Rats team that has never stopped competing and just proved they can beat the best on the road. That is not a comfortable position to hold when the commissioner is adding All-Star shortstops in June.

SIDD FINCH. Four hundred and sixty-eight points. I remember when we all laughed at Sidd Finch in February. Unranked keeper, Bobby Witt paired with question marks, supposed to be the league’s sad story. And here they are in Week 10 posting the highest score of any team this period with deGrom and Alcantara going back-to-back at zero ERA. I am not laughing at Sidd Finch anymore. At 5-5 they are one hot streak away from relevance and every team in the playoff race knows it.

THE MIDLAND RED WEASEL DOGS. I need to say this clearly and I need people to hear it. Noah Cameron pitched thirteen innings. Fifteen strikeouts. Point-six-nine ERA. Fifty-seven-and-a-half points. That is the week’s best individual pitching performance in MLBLONGBALL. His team lost by A HUNDRED POINTS. Because Sidd Finch happened to have their best week of the season on the same week that Cameron was also having his best week. That is the kind of injustice that makes you question the structure of the universe. The Dogs’ pitching deserves a better offense. This has been true for months. The Dogs’ record (4-6) is a CRIME against Cameron and Paul Skenes and Will Warren. Someone on that roster needs to start hitting the baseball before this season becomes a tragedy instead of a disappointment.

The Sons of Pitches won their THIRD game in four weeks. Matt Chapman went 8-for-21 with three home runs and fourteen RBI. Chapman is their best hitter this week and HE’S THE THIRD BASEMAN. Yordan Alvarez added forty-nine-and-a-half. Jake Bauers forty-four-and-a-half. Three hitters in the top seven of the weekly leaderboard for a team that is THREE AND SEVEN. I don’t know if the Sons can make the playoffs. I know they are one of the most interesting teams in the league right now and nobody is covering them because the record says they can’t. The record might be wrong.

The Troutdale Gales scored two hundred and fifty-two points. I genuinely cannot explain this. Mike Trout plays for them. Elly De La Cruz plays for them. I choose to believe there is a rational explanation and I am simply not aware of it. I hope that explanation presents itself before July because at 4-6 they are staring at irrelevance from a very short distance. The clock is not their friend right now. It is not their friend at all.
