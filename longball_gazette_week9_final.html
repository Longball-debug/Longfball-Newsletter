Week 9 Final — May 25–31, 2026 — Scoring Period 9
CASCADES REACH ESCAPE VELOCITY; MISIOROWSKI REWRITES THE RECORD BOOK; RATS SLIDE TO EIGHTH
Jacob Misiorowski threw 72 points into the Troutdale Cascades' week and the rest of the league watched the gap grow. Acuña scored 65 and the BANK still lost. Ohtani scored 52 and the Rats still lost. This is Week 9, where the numbers stopped making sense and started making history.
By the Longball Gazette Wire Staff
League Headquarters — Saturday, May 31, 2026

There is a standard unit of measurement in MLBLONGBALL for how good a pitching performance has to be to decide a matchup. Call it thirty-five points — enough to swing a close game, enough to be the difference between a win and a loss in a week where everything else is roughly even. Jacob Misiorowski of the Troutdale Cascades did not deliver thirty-five points in Week 9. He delivered seventy-two. That is more than double the standard. That is the highest individual pitching performance of the 2026 MLBLONGBALL season, and it single-handedly propelled the Cascades to a 532.333-point week — itself the highest team total of the season — against Goldy Goldy Gone, a team that had no earthly reason to lose by seventy-four points and did so anyway.

The cruelty of this particular week was spread generously across the standings. Ronald Acuña Jr. posted 65 fantasy points for the BANK of Texas — the highest individual hitter output in the entire league — and the BANK lost, falling to the Midland Red Weasel Dogs by 19.333. Shohei Ohtani posted 52 points for the Desert Rats and the Rats lost to the Troutdale Gales by 44.666. In the same week that Misiorowski was rewriting the record book, the league's two most expensive keepers went for a combined 117 points and their teams went a combined 0-2. Fantasy baseball is not always fair. This week it was not even trying.

The Cascades now sit 8-1 on the season, the only team in MLBLONGBALL above .600, on a seven-game winning streak that shows no meaningful signs of ending. Their season pitching total (2,227.833 points) leads the league by over 350 points. They have won with the wire and without it, with dominant individual performances and without them. At this point in the season, the Cascades are not just the best team in MLBLONGBALL. They are operating in a different register entirely.

The BANK of Texas Problem, Stated Simply

The BANK of Texas leads the entire league in season hitting points with 1,965. They are 4-5. Their pitching total (1,697.167 pts) ranks fourth. Acuña delivered 65 points this week and Ben Rice added 59.5. The BANK scored 464.167 in a losing effort. At some point the question stops being about effort and starts being about construction. Something in the BANK's roster is not converting elite hitting into wins at the rate it should. The pitching, specifically, is costing them games that the offense is winning on paper.

Desert Rats: Eighth Place Is a Problem

The Desert Rats are 6-3 on the season and eighth in scoring points (1,997.5 hitting, 7th; 1,659.667 pitching, 5th). They hold 6 wins that suggest competitiveness and a points-scored total that suggests something is bleeding. Their –102 GIDP is the worst in the league by 8 points — at –2 per double play, that represents 204 points surrendered to the worst possible outcome. The commissioner has made transactions. He has not yet fixed the lineup composition problem that keeps converting Ohtani's production into double-play outs.

The Week One Number Explained Everything
By Joe Falls  ·  J.G. Taylor Spink Award Winner, 2001  ·  The Longball Gazette

Seventy-two. That is the number I keep coming back to. Jacob Misiorowski of the Troutdale Cascades threw seventy-two fantasy points in one week. Fourteen innings. An ERA of 0.64. Twenty strikeouts. Two quality starts. I have covered baseball in this league since 2008 and I will tell you plainly: that is the finest individual pitching performance I have seen in a single scoring period. Not just this year. In the history of this league.

The Cascades are 8-1. They are on a seven-game winning streak. Their pitching total on the season (2,227.833) leads the league by 359 points. And in the week they posted the highest single-team score in 2026 league history, Braxton Ashcraft added 53.17 points alongside Misiorowski, and Bryan Woo threw seven innings at 0.00 ERA and contributed 36.5. Three Cascades pitchers in the top ten. Three. The same week their ace posted seventy-two.

Now I want to say something about Ronald Acuña Jr. because this man deserves an apology from the standings. He scored 65 fantasy points. The highest individual hitter performance in the league this week by any player on any team. Five home runs. Ten RBI. And his team lost by nineteen points to the Midland Red Weasel Dogs, whose best hitter was James Wood at 46. Acuña and Ben Rice combined for 124.5 points. Together. In a loss. If you can look at that number and tell me the BANK's roster construction is not failing its best players, I will listen carefully and then disagree.

The Desert Rats. Six and three. Shohei Ohtani posted 52 points this week. Fifty-two. Fourth-best hitter in the entire league. His team lost to the Troutdale Gales by forty-four points. I have been saying since April that the –102 GIDP is the number that defines this franchise's season and I am running out of new ways to say it. Two hundred and four points have been surrendered to double plays. At a scoring system that pays Ohtani's 52-point weeks four points per home run, you cannot afford to give two points back on every ground ball that turns into a double play. The commissioner has made transactions. He has not made the transaction. Corey Seager is a good add. The double-play problem requires a different kind of solution.

Two things I want to note in closing. The Sons of Pitches made wire moves this week. Brendan Donovan. Spencer Steer. Multiple adds on Wednesday afternoon. I do not know whether this changes their season. I know it is the first time in weeks the franchise has acknowledged that the roster they drafted is not the roster that wins games in May. That is a start. Whether it is more than a start, the final three weeks will tell us.

And Ithaca Spartans: two consecutive wins. Jonathan Aranda gave them 44.5 points. They beat the Sons of Pitches and before that they beat someone else and now they are 3-6 with a little momentum and a pitching total that is last in the league by 395 points. The wins are real. The structural problem is also real. Those two things can both be true at the same time.

On the Difference Between Winning and Scoring
By Mitch Albom  ·  Detroit Free Press  ·  The Longball Gazette

There is a distinction in this sport — in any team sport — between a team that is performing well and a team that is winning. The week of May 25 through May 31 made that distinction as clear as it has ever been in MLBLONGBALL. The BANK of Texas scored 464 fantasy points in a loss. Ronald Acuña Jr. delivered 65. Ben Rice delivered 59.5. These are performances that, in isolation, represent excellence. They are also performances that, in the context of a system, represent a gap between individual output and team outcome that is widening rather than closing.

The question worth asking about the BANK is not whether their players are performing. They are. The question is whether the roster around those performances is completing the circuit. A team that scores more points than any other in the league on offense (1,965 hitting points, first overall) and is 4-5 is a team where something between the offense and the win column is absorbing production without returning it. In the BANK's case, I believe that something is pitching efficiency. Their 1,697 pitching points rank fourth, which sounds adequate until you note that the teams ahead of them in the standings have pitching totals that are proportionally higher. The offense sets a high floor. The pitching has not built a ceiling to match it.

The Desert Rats' situation is a mirror image told from a different angle. Where the BANK scores points and cannot convert them to wins because of pitching, the Rats have the keepers — Ohtani, Tucker — and cannot convert their individual performances into wins because of a lineup construction problem that has compounded for nine weeks into a –102 GIDP total. Two hundred and four points surrendered to double plays. That is not bad luck. That is a pattern.

And then there is Jacob Misiorowski. Seventy-two points. I have been writing about sports long enough to know that the singular performance is often the one that breaks the pattern of analysis. You cannot build a model for seventy-two points from one starter. You can only witness it and acknowledge that the Troutdale Cascades, who have now been the best team in MLBLONGBALL since March, have found a way to produce individual excellence on top of systemic excellence — and that the combination of those two things is what makes them, at 8-1 with a seven-game winning streak, not just the league's best team, but its most complete one.

The Sons of Pitches made wire moves this week. That is worth noting, not because it changes the season, but because it suggests acknowledgment. In my experience, acknowledgment precedes correction. Whether the correction arrives in time is a different question. Yordan Alvarez (48 points this week) and Jeremy Peña (45 points) have been performing at levels that deserve better teammates. Sometimes recognition of that fact, expressed in action on the wire, is the beginning of something real. We will see.

Big Lou’s Corner: I Cannot Explain Seventy-Two Points And I Am Not Going To Try
By Lou Palmieri, Baseball Lifer  ·  The Longball Gazette

Jacob Misiorowski. Seventy-two fantasy points. I’ve been watching fantasy baseball since before some of you were born and I want to be precise: I have never seen a seventy-two-point pitching week. Not once. That is not a normal output. That is a man who was, for one scoring period, physically incapable of allowing an earned run or missing the strike zone when it mattered. Fourteen innings. 0.64 ERA. TWENTY STRIKEOUTS. And then Braxton Ashcraft came in at fifty-three and change and Bryan Woo added thirty-six at 0.00 and the Cascades scored FIVE HUNDRED AND THIRTY-TWO POINTS. Against Goldy Goldy Gone! Against the second-place team! By seventy-four points!

The Troutdale Cascades are 8-1. They have a seven-game winning streak. Their pitching total leads the league by THREE HUNDRED AND FIFTY-NINE POINTS. Do you understand what that means? It means the second-best pitching staff in this league — the Newberry Indians, who are excellent — is 359 points behind. That is not a gap. That is a different sport being played in the same league.

Now. The BANK of Texas. Ronald Acuña scored sixty-five points. SIXTY-FIVE. The highest individual hitter in the entire league this week. Ben Rice scored fifty-nine and a half. These two men combined for a hundred and twenty-four points. And the team LOST. By nineteen. To the Midland Red Weasel Dogs who are fourth in the standings. I want someone at the BANK to look at a hundred and twenty-four points from two hitters and then look at the final score and tell me what the plan is. Because the plan is not working and it has not been working for nine weeks and at some point having the best hitting in the league needs to produce something other than the eighth-best record.

Desert Rats. Shohei Ohtani. Fifty-two points. He batted .318. He hit home runs. His team lost to the Troutdale Gales by forty-four points and now they are eighth in FPts scored while sitting 6-3 in the win column. I said since February this team wins the championship. I am not changing my prediction. But I am adding a condition: the –102 GIDP has to stop. Two hundred and four points surrendered to double plays over nine weeks. Corey Seager is a good add. He does not fix ground balls. The commissioner needs to look at every player in that lineup and ask the simple question: does this person hit the ball in the air? Because the ones who don't are killing the season quietly, two points at a time, every night.

The Newberry Indians beat Houston by fourteen points because Ben Brown pitched 51.5 points at 1.38 ERA and someone claimed him in a Vickrey auction for a bid of 153 and paid one. ONE REAL DOLLAR for the pitcher who won the game. That is this format working exactly as designed. That is why I love the Vickrey system and that is why the Newberry Indians have 335 season SVH and the second-best pitching total in the league. They are paying attention at midnight. Are you?

Sons of Pitches made moves this week. Brendan Donovan. Spencer Steer. Yordan Alvarez has forty-eight points in a losing effort and Jeremy Peña has forty-five and they lost to ITHACA SPARTANS by fifty-six points because the pitching total for the Sons of Pitches on the season is one thousand two hundred and forty-nine points and the next-to-last team has a hundred and thirty-one more. You cannot win with that pitching staff. The moves are better late than never. They are also very late. Three weeks to go. Good luck.
