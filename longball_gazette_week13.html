Week 13 Final — June 22–28, 2026
FIVE UPSETS IN SIX GAMES; DOGS OUTLAST GOLDY; RATS AND CASCADES BOTH STUMBLE
The most chaotic week of the 2026 MLBLONGBALL season: five upsets, a 232-point Scorpions bounceback, and the Midland Red Weasel Dogs walking off Goldy Goldy Gone 384.33–381.5 behind Junior Caminero's 70-point masterpiece. The playoff picture just got a lot more interesting.
By the Longball Gazette Wire Staff
League Headquarters — Sunday, June 28, 2026

Week 13 was the kind of week that reminds you why fantasy baseball runs thirteen weeks before the standings mean anything definitive. Five of six games produced an upset. The Newberry Indians walked into Troutdale and handed the Cascades a 95-point loss. The Tempe Scorpions, coming off a 218-point catastrophe in Week 12, responded by beating the Desert Rats 450 to 440.5. The Houston Gamblers knocked off Sidd Finch. The Sons of Pitches took down The BANK of Texas again. And the Midland Red Weasel Dogs defeated Goldy Goldy Gone 384.33–381.5, with Junior Caminero's 70-point hitting performance carrying the load while Robbie Ray threw sixteen scoreless innings to seal it.

The only team that did what it was supposed to do was the Troutdale Gales, who posted 488.67 points against the Ithaca Spartans and won convincingly. Three pitchers in the top seven of the weekly leaderboard — Foster Griffin (62.17 pts), Brandon Woodruff (50.33 pts), and Jesus Luzardo (45.33 pts) — and the Gales still come out of Week 13 at 5-8. The pitching is not the problem. It never has been. The problem was the first half of the season, and no amount of rotation dominance can retroactively fix April.

The playoff picture entering Week 14 is as compressed as it has been all season. Troutdale Cascades lead at 10-3. The Desert Rats sit second at 9-4, one game back. Newberry Indians hold third at 8-5. Then the Goldy Goldy Gone and Midland Red Weasel Dogs share fourth at 7-6 — both inside the playoff line, both surging. Below the cutline: Scorpions, Gamblers, and Sidd Finch all at 6-7. One game is all that separates in or out. With top six making playoffs and five weeks remaining, nothing is settled.

Deadline Deals: Goldy and Scorpions Make Their Moves

Ahead of the trade deadline, the Goldy Goldy Gone and Tempe Scorpions completed a 2-for-2 swap. Goldy sends Kevin Gausman (SP, TOR) and Brooks Lee (2B/3B/SS, MIN) to the Scorpions in exchange for Jakob Junis (RP, TEX) and Otto Lopez (2B/SS, MIA). The Scorpions add a veteran frontline starter in Gausman as they push toward the playoff cutline from 6-7. Goldy acquires bullpen flexibility, though they now close the week at 7-6 after dropping a 2.83-point heartbreaker to the Dogs.

Caminero Carries the Dogs to a Crucial Win

Junior Caminero (TB, Midland Red Weasel Dogs) put up 70 fantasy points — the week's top hitting performance by a wide margin — as the Dogs outlasted Goldy Goldy Gone 384.33–381.5. Robbie Ray added 59.5 pitching points on 16 scoreless innings. The Dogs move to 7-6, firmly in the top half of the standings and inside the playoff cutline. It is the kind of week that turns a season around.

Five Upsets and What the Standings Are Telling Us
By Joe Falls  ·  J.G. Taylor Spink Award Winner, 2001  ·  The Longball Gazette

Five upsets in six games. I have been covering this league since the beginning and I want to tell you that a week like this is not random. It is not noise. It is what happens when the middle of a league is tightly packed, when rosters are being shuffled at the deadline, and when the teams at the bottom of the standings have enough talent to beat the teams at the top on a given week. Week 13 of the 2026 MLBLONGBALL season was a week where the standings said one thing and the scoreboard said another. And the scoreboard won.

The Cascades are still first. They lost by 95 points to a team that is 8-5. The Rats are still second. They lost by 9.5 to a team that scored 218 last week. Those two sentences together tell you what this league is: competitive, unpredictable, and unforgiving. Both teams have earned their positions through 13 weeks of consistent performance. One bad week from either of them, at the wrong moment, against the wrong opponent, can still cost them.

What I keep coming back to is the Newberry Indians. Eight and five. They beat the first-place team on the road by 95 points. Luis Garcia Jr. was claimed off the wire this week for $156 and went out and hit 61 fantasy points. Their pitching staff has logged more innings than any team in MLBLONGBALL this season — 2,260 innings and counting. They are not a fluke. They are a team that is well-constructed and peaking at exactly the right time.

The Midland Red Weasel Dogs are a story that deserves more attention than it is getting. A week ago they were 6-7 and outside the playoff cutline. This week Junior Caminero hit 70 points, Robbie Ray threw 16 scoreless innings for 59.5 more, and they beat Goldy Goldy Gone 384.33–381.5 to move to 7-6. That is the most complete roster performance of the week on a team that needed it badly. The Dogs are inside the top six for the first time in weeks.

The four-team cluster at 6-7 is the most interesting story in the league right now. Scorpions, Gamblers, and Sidd Finch all with the same record, all separated by tiebreaker, all with legitimate cases for why they belong in the playoffs and legitimate reasons to be concerned they won't get there. The Scorpions hold sixth on tiebreaker. The Gamblers are seventh. Sidd Finch is eighth. One game separates in or out. Everything happens in Week 14.

Caminero, the Dogs, and the Weight of a Week
By Mitch Albom  ·  Detroit Free Press  ·  The Longball Gazette

Junior Caminero put up 70 fantasy points this week. He hit seven home runs, drove in fifteen runs, scored eight, and was for seven days the most productive fantasy hitter in this league by a margin that was not close. His team won. I want to start there because it is easy to write about dominant individual performances that end in defeat — and there have been several in this league this season — but Caminero's week was something different. He carried the Midland Red Weasel Dogs to a win that moves them to 7-6 and into the playoff picture, and that matters.

Robbie Ray threw sixteen innings without allowing a run. Fifty-nine and a half fantasy points from a starting pitcher in a single week is a remarkable performance by any standard. The Dogs had the week's top hitter and the week's second-best pitcher on the same roster in the same game. They won by 2.83 points. Not because they needed every one of those points to survive, but because Goldy Goldy Gone posted 381.5 points, which on most weeks would be a competitive total, and this week wasn't enough. That is the level this game is being played at right now.

The Troutdale Gales put three pitchers in the top seven of the weekly leaderboard. Foster Griffin, Brandon Woodruff, Jesus Luzardo. Combined pitching points: 157.83. The Gales posted 488.67 points, the week's highest team score. They won by 222 points. They are 5-8. That is still the number. Five and eight. A record that cannot be fixed by a week of excellent pitching and cannot be explained by a week of poor hitting, because the Gales rank second in cumulative hitting points in this entire league. The math of the first half of the season is permanent. What the Gales are doing right now is beautiful and too late.

What strikes me about Week 13 is not the chaos, though there was plenty of it. What strikes me is that the teams that needed to win — the Dogs, the Scorpions, the Gamblers — all won. And the teams that were expected to win — the Rats, the Cascades, Goldy, Sidd Finch — all lost. Whether that is momentum or variance, we will find out in Week 14.

Big Lou’s Corner: This League Has Lost Its Mind and I Am Here for Every Second of It
By Lou Palmieri, Baseball Lifer  ·  The Longball Gazette

FIVE UPSETS IN SIX GAMES. Let me say that again for the people in the back. FIVE. UPSETS. IN. SIX. GAMES. Do you understand what had to happen for that to occur? The Scorpions, who scored 218 points last week, had to beat the Desert Rats. The Sons, who are 5-8, had to beat the BANK. The Indians had to walk into Troutdale and win by 95 points. The Gamblers had to beat Sidd Finch at Sidd Finch's house. AND THE WEASEL DOGS HAD TO BEAT GOLDY WITH CAMINERO AND ROBBIE RAY. All of that happened in the same week. I have been in this league a long time. I have never seen a week like this. Not once.

JUNIOR CAMINERO. Seventy points. Seven home runs. Fifteen RBI. I have to keep saying the numbers out loud because they don't feel real even when I look at the box score. His team won. FINALLY. A man puts up the biggest hitting week of the 2026 season and his team wins the game. I was very concerned that Caminero was going to become the poster child for dominant individual performances that end in defeat, like what happened to Bryan Reynolds a few weeks back. Instead he is the story of a Dogs team that needed this win desperately and got it by 2.83 points.

I want to talk about the Scorpions for a minute because WHAT IS HAPPENING. 218 points. Two hundred and eighteen. That was last week. This week: 450 points, Michael Wacha with 51 points and 14.2 innings, Vargas and O'Hearn both in the top five of the batting leaderboard, and an upset of the Desert Rats on the road. That is a 232-point improvement in seven days. I don't know what broke in Week 12 and I don't know what got fixed for Week 13, but the Scorpions are holding the sixth playoff spot on tiebreaker right now and they just traded for Kevin Gausman. The Gamblers and Sidd Finch are watching this very carefully.

And let me close with the Gales. Because Griffin and Woodruff and Luzardo and 488 points and 5-8 and the most confounding roster in MLBLONGBALL history. I have watched teams win with bad pitching. I have watched teams win with bad hitting. I have never watched a team go 5-8 with the second-best hitting total and the worst pitching total in a league and then suddenly develop three elite starters at the same time in June. This franchise is a mystery wrapped in a riddle wrapped in a very good rotation. I cannot explain it. I have stopped trying. I am just watching.
