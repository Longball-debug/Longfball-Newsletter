Week 12 Final — June 15–21, 2026
RATS POST 504; CASCADES HOLD ON; SONS STEAL ONE BY A WHISKER
The Desert Rats put up the week's highest score by a wide margin, Logan Gilbert was untouchable, and the gap between first and second place remains exactly one game. Meanwhile the most dramatic finish of the 2026 season played out between Sidd Finch and the Sons of Pitches, separated at the final buzzer by two points.
By the Longball Gazette Wire Staff
League Headquarters — Sunday, June 21, 2026

The Desert Rats are not going away. Sitting one game behind the Troutdale Cascades entering Week 12, the commissioner's squad responded by posting 504.33 points — the week's highest total by a comfortable margin — to dispose of the Ithaca Spartans 504.33 to 250.17. Logan Gilbert led the charge with 63.67 fantasy points: 13.1 innings pitched, a 1.35 ERA, and 18 strikeouts. That is not a good week from a starting pitcher. That is a dominant one. The Rats move to 9-3. The Cascades also won. The gap is still one game. And that is exactly how this league's best rivalry is supposed to feel.

The Troutdale Cascades, for their part, did what first-place teams are supposed to do: they won. A 490.50-point effort against The BANK of Texas (405.83) keeps them at 10-2 and one game ahead of a Rats team that keeps refusing to fall back. The race at the top has been a one-game affair for what feels like a month. Cascades. Rats. One game. Every week it resets and every week it holds.

If those were the headliners, the Sons of Pitches provided the story of the week. In what may be the closest game in MLBLONGBALL 2026, the Sons edged Sidd Finch 441.50 to 439.50 — a two-point margin that will be discussed long after this season ends. Sidd entered the week's final day needing just three points to win. They did not get them. Pete Crow-Armstrong (58.5 pts) put up the week's best individual hitting performance for Finch, and it wasn't enough. The Sons survive. Sidd Finch stares at a loss that came down to two fantasy points.

Scorpions Hit Season Low; Goldy Picks Up a Needed Win

The Tempe Scorpions posted 218 points — the week's lowest score and almost certainly the lowest output by any team in a single week this season. The Goldy Goldy Gone squad needed only 332.83 to win, and they did. For the Scorpions, at 5-7 and sinking, the 218-point week is the kind of result that ends playoff conversations. Goldy moves to 7-5 and stays in the hunt in the Blue Division.

Dogs Finally Get One; Gamblers Come Up Short Again

The Midland Red Weasel Dogs defeated the Houston Gamblers 330.17 to 254.83, a result that gives the Dogs a 6-6 record and some breathing room in the standings. The Gamblers fall to 5-7 and are now three games below .500 with limited weeks to recover. Cam Schlittler (38.5 pts, Midland) and Louis Varland (34.67 pts, Midland) gave the Dogs a pitching edge that the Gamblers' offense couldn't overcome.

One Game. Every Week. That's the Race.
By Joe Falls  ·  J.G. Taylor Spink Award Winner, 2001  ·  The Longball Gazette

The gap between the Troutdale Cascades and the Desert Rats has been one game for what feels like half the season now. One game in April. One game in May. One game entering Week 12. One game after it. I have been covering fantasy baseball for a long time and I am telling you: two teams running this close for this long is unusual. It means something about both of them.

The Cascades are 10-2. They posted 490 points this week against a BANK of Texas team that put up 405. They have two pitchers in the top seven of the weekly leaderboard and two hitters in the top ten. Their cumulative FPts total is the highest in the league by 496 points over the second-place Rats. They are the best team in MLBLONGBALL. That is not an opinion. That is what the numbers say.

And then there are the Desert Rats, who went out and posted 504 points, which was the week's highest total, and Logan Gilbert threw 13.1 innings with an ERA of 1.35 and 18 strikeouts. That is a dominant pitching performance on a dominant scoring week from a team that is 9-3 and on a three-game winning streak. The Rats are not fading. They are not content with second place. They have been one game back for a month and they keep refusing to let it become two.

I want to say something about the Sidd Finch situation because 439.50 points is a legitimate team effort and they lost by two. Pete Crow-Armstrong gave them 58.5 points — the week's best individual hitting performance in the league. Nolan McLean went 7 innings and allowed nothing. That is a complete team week. They lost. Two points separated a 6-6 record from a 7-5 record in the Red Division standings. Sidd Finch has had bad weeks this season and they have had brilliant weeks. Week 12 was a brilliant week that they lost anyway. That is a different kind of hard.

The Tempe Scorpions posted 218 points. I have been doing this long enough to know that 218 points in a single week is a configuration problem, not a talent problem. The talent is there. The Scorpions lead the White Division despite a 5-7 record because the White Division has been collectively poor this year. But 218 points is the kind of week that derails seasons. They face the Desert Rats in Week 13. The projections are not kind. For the Scorpions to stay relevant, they need to find 200 additional points from somewhere before the week ends.

Two Points and What They Mean
By Mitch Albom  ·  Detroit Free Press  ·  The Longball Gazette

There is a game being played in this league that will be remembered, if such things are remembered, for the fact that it ended with a margin of two fantasy points. Sidd Finch scored 439.50. The Sons of Pitches scored 441.50. Between those two numbers is the difference between a winning record and a losing one, between momentum and disappointment, between a week that felt like progress and a week that will feel like loss for some time.

Pete Crow-Armstrong delivered 58.5 points for Sidd Finch. If you are building a case for the week's best individual performance, you start with Crow-Armstrong. He was excellent. His team had 439 points at the final whistle. And they lost. That is the nature of this game — individual brilliance does not guarantee collective success, and a week that deserved to go differently went differently anyway.

I want to think about what it means to be the Sons of Pitches right now. Four wins and eight losses is a hard record to carry. But they have now won two of their last three games, and the most recent victory came against 439.50 points of opposition. Zack Wheeler threw 11.2 innings and gave up three runs. Joey Cantillo added eight more innings at 1.13 ERA. That is not a team that is losing. That is a team that is 4-8 because the first month of the season happened the way it happened, and what comes after that is a different story written by different weeks.

The Desert Rats posted 504 points. I do not want to only see the number, though the number is significant. What matters is what the number came from: Logan Gilbert choosing Week 12 to throw the best individual pitching performance in the league this period. A team that is three wins behind the Cascades in the all-time season standings and one game behind them in the current one. A commissioner's squad that has been climbing toward something all year and keeps arriving exactly one game short. The Rats are not chasing the Cascades anymore. They are running alongside them. The last month of the season will tell us which one is actually faster.

The Troutdale Gales are 4-8 with the best cumulative hitting total in the league. That is a sentence I keep returning to because I cannot resolve the contradiction it contains. Kyle Schwarber gave them 48.5 points this week in a losing effort. Somewhere in the gap between what the Gales' hitting numbers promise and what their record delivers is a pitching staff that has cost them somewhere between four and six wins this season. I do not know how to feel about a team whose offense is this good and whose season looks like this. I think they know exactly how to feel. I think it has been this way for months.

Big Lou’s Corner: Gilbert Was a Freight Train and Nobody Was Getting Off the Tracks
By Lou Palmieri, Baseball Lifer  ·  The Longball Gazette

SIXTY-THREE POINT SIXTY-SEVEN. That is what Logan Gilbert did for the Desert Rats this week and I need everyone to sit with that number for a moment. Thirteen-and-a-third innings. One-point-three-five ERA. Eighteen strikeouts. The week's best pitching performance in MLBLONGBALL by a margin that is not close. And he did it while his team was posting FIVE HUNDRED AND FOUR TOTAL POINTS. That is not a coincidence. That is an ace doing ace things at the exact moment his team needed a statement week. The Rats went out and made a statement. Gilbert signed it, sealed it, and delivered it personally.

Now let me talk about the Cascades because 10-2 is a number that deserves to be said out loud. TEN AND TWO. In a twelve-team competitive fantasy league where everybody is trying, ten wins through twelve weeks is extraordinary. Chase Burns at 0.90 ERA. Bryan Woo at 0.00 ERA. Two pitchers in the top seven of the weekly leaderboard AND two hitters in the top ten batting leaderboard. The BANK of Texas put up 405 points and lost by 85. You want to know how good the Cascades are? That is how good the Cascades are.

SONS OF PITCHES WIN BY TWO POINTS. I was not going to let this issue close without addressing this directly. The Sons are 4-8. They have Zack Wheeler throwing 11.2 innings at 1.54 ERA. They have Joey Cantillo at 8 innings, 1.13 ERA. They beat Sidd Finch who had the week's best hitter and 439.50 total points. They beat them by TWO POINTS. I am not saying the Sons are a playoff team. I am not saying the 4-8 record is misleading. I am saying that when this league sits down to write the story of the 2026 season, the Sons-Sidd game from Week 12 is going to be in the chapter about things that matter. Two points. That is all.

Tempe Scorpions: 218 points. I am going to give them the benefit of the doubt and assume something went catastrophically wrong with their lineup. I have to believe that. Because the alternative — that a team in contention for a playoff spot, however tenuous, simply produced 218 points in a full week of major league baseball — is a scenario I cannot fully accept. They face the Desert Rats next week. The Rats just posted 504. I hope for the Scorpions' sake that what happened in Week 12 is an anomaly and not a preview of what the rest of their season looks like.
