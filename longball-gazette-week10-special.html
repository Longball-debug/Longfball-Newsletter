Composite Analysis — Injury Load × Remaining Schedule

The Two-Headed Monster:
Injuries and the Road Ahead

Nine periods left. Twelve rosters bleeding. The question isn't just who's hurt — it's whether the teams that are hurt can survive the schedule they still have to play.

Newberry Indians are carrying the heaviest injury load in the league and still lead their division. BANK of Texas has the most dangerous combination of injuries and schedule difficulty among playoff contenders. Troutdale Cascades have the easiest path and the least to worry about. The rest of the league falls somewhere in between — and the gap between where they are and where they need to be is closing fast.

We built a composite pressure score for every team by combining three factors: current record (where you stand relative to the playoff cutline), remaining schedule difficulty (opponent win percentages across SP 11–19), and injury impact (weighted by each player's fantasy value in LONGBALL scoring). The result is a single number that tells you who's in real trouble and who has room to breathe.

The Stories That Matter

The composite scores split naturally into four conversations. The contenders who are bleeding but still standing. The bubble teams where a single return or absence decides everything. The chaserteams who need to run the table against tough schedules. And the two teams whose injuries, paradoxically, don't explain their record at all.

No Injuries, No Excuses

Two teams sit at 3–7 with no reported injuries on their board. That makes them the most analytically simple cases in LONGBALL right now — and the most brutal.

3–7 · Composite: 5.79

Now they face the hardest remaining schedule in the league (.556 SOS) with no structural excuse and no time left. The Bottom Line
In the Clear
Troutdale Cascades · Desert Rats
Best records. Softest schedules. Lightest injury loads. Barring collapse, both are in the playoffs. Both could earn byes. The only question is seeding.
The Dangerous Combination
BANK of Texas · Sidd Finch
Both at .500. Both with significant injury losses to pitching and offense. Both facing hard schedules. One of them — probably both — will not make the playoffs unless key players return this week.
Injuries That Can Be Survived
Goldy Goldy Gone · Newberry Indians
Both carry heavy injury loads. Both have enough depth and schedule cushion to absorb it — for now. Goldy's calendar is the easiest of any contender. Newberry's depth has already defied expectations twice.
Already Eliminated by the Math
Ithaca · Sons of Pitches · Tempe · Gales
Not impossible, but requiring near-perfection against tough schedules while multiple contenders need to simultaneously collapse. Plan for 2027.
